New Job: Research Fellow on the EU SUPERHUB Project 21.08.2012

At the beginning of April I joined the SUPERHUB project, an EU IP project staffed by a Europe-wide consortium of twenty commercial & academic partners. SUPERHUB (SUstainable and PERsuasive Human Users moBility in future cities) is financed by the European Commission under Grant agreement no.: 289067. The aim is to provide a user-centric, integrated approach to providing multi-modal, smart, metropolitan mobility systems. An open platform will be designed & tested that is able to combine in real time all mobility offers from the relevant stakeholders together with a set of enabling mobility services able to address users mobility needs, to redesign transport route options and to foster behavioural changes.

It is too early to say how my role will develop in this project but what excites me are the opportunites to do digital behaviour management, specifically persuasion, in a way that aims for long-term and sustainable behaviour change. A second point of interest for me at least is to observe how an ostensibly open-platform is built from scratch as an open-source project and to experience first-hand the tensions and issues that arise when entrenched, historically closed commercial interests meet open approaches to science, research, and business.

Simon Wells.
