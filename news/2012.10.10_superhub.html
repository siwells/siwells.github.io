Deliverable: SUPERHUB D6.1 - Multi-Access Platform Front-End 10.10.2012

Abstract: The SUPERHUB project aims at realizing a new services mobility framework supporting an integrated and eco-efficient use of multi-modal mobility systems in an urban setting. SUPERHUB provides a user-centric, integrated approach to multi-modal smart metropolitan mobility systems by means of an open platform able to combine in real time various mobility offers and provide a set of mobility services able to address user needs, promote user participation and foster environmentally friendly and energy–efficient behavioural changes.
Deliverable D6.1, "Multi-Access Platform Front-End" describes the process of designing the initial SUPERHUB user interface. This process has been guided by a user-centred design methodology that has incorporated both participatory design workshops with target user- groups and expert heuristic evaluations by usability experts. Additional to the design process, the SUPERHUB front-end must be underpinned by use of appropriate software engineering tools, techniques, and technologies. Therefore the background on underpinning technologies to support the development of nomadic interfaces, contextual interventions, multi-lingual interfaces, behaviour tracking, data storage, and API design are also presented here, together with recommendations for subsequent development of the front-end prototype.
D6.1 should be read in conjunction with D6.2 "Design of the Persuasive Service GUIs & Interaction" which will allow a more fully realised picture of the SUPERHUB front-end functionality to be built especially in relation to how behaviour modification and persuasive elements of the interface are underpinned by WP6 functionality.
